Section 6 · Putting It All Together

Step-by-Step Trade Planning

Trade planning means deciding everything about a trade before you place it — entry price, stop loss price, take profit price, and position size — all determined in advance, in a calm, analytical state. Not in the heat of the moment with a live position bleeding against you.

Think of a trade plan like directions for a road trip: you know your starting point, your destination, and what you will do if something unexpected happens along the way. You do not start driving and figure out the route as you go. Trading without a plan is exactly that — driving blind in the dark, hoping you end up somewhere profitable.

Key Terms

Trade Plan — A specific, written plan for a single trade: instrument, direction, entry, SL, TP, and position size — all decided before placing the order.
HTF (Higher Timeframe) — The larger timeframe used for determining bias and locating key zones. Typically Daily or H4.
LTF (Lower Timeframe) — The smaller timeframe used for precise entry triggers. Typically H1, M15, or M5.
Top-Down Analysis — The process of starting with the highest timeframe and progressively zooming in to the entry timeframe.
📊 Simple Trade Plan Template

Instrument: EURUSD
Bias: Bullish (Daily uptrend intact)
Entry: 1.0850 (after H1 bullish confirmation candle at demand zone)
Stop Loss: 1.0815 (below zone low + buffer)
Take Profit: 1.0950 (next H4 resistance)
Position Size: 0.3 lots ($10,000 account, 1% risk, 35-pip SL)
R:R: 1:2.9

Everything decided before clicking entry. No in-trade decisions required.

The complete top-down analytical process — six steps from blank chart to filled trade plan:

Step 1 — Weekly/Daily: Determine the macro trend.
Is price making higher highs and higher lows (bullish)? Lower highs and lower lows (bearish)? Ranging between two horizontal levels? Write down the bias in one word: bullish, bearish, or ranging. If you cannot determine this clearly, wait — a clear bias will form.

Step 2 — H4: Identify the key zone.
Where is significant institutional buying or selling interest most likely? Mark demand zones, supply zones, and order blocks on H4. These are the targets. Set price alerts at the zone edges so you know when price arrives without having to watch the screen constantly.

Step 3 — H4: Wait for price to reach the zone.
Do not enter because price is "near" the zone. Price must trade into it. Walk away from the screen. The market will come to you — or it will not reach your zone and you skip the setup entirely. Both outcomes are acceptable.

Step 4 — H1/M15: Look for the trigger.
Once price enters the H4 zone: Did a nearby swing high or low get swept? Has a CHoCH formed on H1 or M15 in the direction of your HTF bias? Is there a strong rejection candle — a bullish engulfing or bearish pin bar — inside the zone? You need at least one of these LTF signals.

Step 5 — Set SL, TP, and position size before entry.
Calculate all three before clicking. SL below the zone structural low (for longs), TP at the next HTF resistance, position size using the formula. Write it down.

Step 6 — Execute.
Market order at the open of the confirmation candle, or a limit order at the midpoint of the trigger candle. Click. Set your SL and TP. Walk away — the trade will either hit your target or your stop, and either outcome was planned for.

💡 Pro Tip

Keep a trade plan document open (a simple text file or spreadsheet) and fill in all six steps before reaching Step 6. If you cannot fill in the entry, SL, and TP with specific numbers, the setup is not clear enough yet. The inability to write a coherent plan is itself the signal to wait.

Institutional-grade trade planning adds premium/discount analysis and multi-timeframe entry refinement:

Premium/Discount Arrays: Before analyzing zones, determine whether price is currently in a "premium" (above the 50% midpoint of the current range — unfavorable for buys) or "discount" (below the midpoint — favorable for buys). This filter alone eliminates many low-probability entries. Buy only in discount zones; sell only in premium zones. The equilibrium point is the 50% Fibonacci retracement of the most recent swing.

Session entry windows: Add a timing constraint to your plan: "Entry only during London (3:00–5:00am EST) or New York (8:00–10:00am EST) session open." Entries in the middle of the New York session or the dead Asian session carry significantly lower probability of a clean, impulsive move from the zone.

Zone entry refinement: Instead of one market order, plan a scaled entry: primary limit order at the top of the demand zone, secondary limit at the midpoint. This improves average entry price and reduces the position size risk of any single fill. If neither fills because price reverses from the zone edge — you still participate at the top.

Advanced Trade Plan Fields

Premium/Discount bias — Confirm price is in the discount zone (for longs) before proceeding.
Entry window — Specific session hours when institutional volume supports the intended move.
Invalidation level — The price at which the entire trade thesis is structurally wrong — distinct from the SL. Re-evaluate before re-entering if invalidation is hit.
Correlation check — Confirm no other open positions share the same macro driver as this trade.

D1 bias: Bullish. BOS upward at $1,950. Current pullback forming higher low.
Premium/Discount: Range low $1,880, high $1,960. Midpoint $1,920. Price at $1,917 = discount. ✓

💡 Pro Tip

Write your trade plan in a physical journal or a digital document — not in your head. The act of writing forces clarity, exposes logical gaps, and creates a record for post-trade review. A plan that cannot be written coherently cannot be executed consistently. "Plan the trade, trade the plan" is the most repeated phrase in professional trading because it solves the most common and expensive problem: in-trade emotional decision-making.

Key Takeaway

Every decision made before market execution is rational; every decision made during a live trade is emotional. Remove in-trade decisions by completing a full written trade plan — entry, SL, TP, position size, session window — before placing any order. Plan the trade, trade the plan.
