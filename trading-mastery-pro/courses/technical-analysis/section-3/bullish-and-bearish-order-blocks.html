Bullish and Bearish Order Blocks

There are two types of order blocks. Understanding both — and when to use each — is the foundation of order block trading.

Bullish Order Block (Long Entry)

  • The last BEARISH (red/down) candle before a strong bullish (up) move
  • Used for LONG (buy) entries when price returns to that candle
  • Located below current price; price needs to pull back DOWN to reach it

Bearish Order Block (Short Entry)

  • The last BULLISH (green/up) candle before a strong bearish (down) move
  • Used for SHORT (sell) entries when price returns to that candle
  • Located above current price; price needs to rally UP to reach it

Identification Checklist (4 Steps)

  1. Find a strong impulsive move — 3 or more large candles all moving in the same direction
  2. Look at the candle immediately BEFORE that impulsive move that goes in the opposite direction
  3. That candle IS the order block
  4. Mark the HIGH and LOW of that candle's body (open to close) — not the wicks

Key Terms

Bullish OB — The last bearish candle before a strong up-move; provides long (buy) entries on retest.
Bearish OB — The last bullish candle before a strong down-move; provides short (sell) entries on retest.
OB Body — The open-to-close range of the OB candle; the precise entry zone for the trade.
OB High / OB Low — The top and bottom of the OB candle body; used to define entry zone and stop placement.
📊 Practical Example

GBPUSD H4: Price falls 80 pips over 3 candles. The candle immediately before that 3-candle drop is a green (bullish) candle with a body from 1.2710 to 1.2724 — that is the bearish OB. Later, when price rallies back up to 1.2710–1.2724, short setups are triggered from that candle's body. Stop is placed above 1.2724 (OB body high) plus a small buffer.

💡 Pro Tip

A memory aid: "OB colour is opposite to trade direction." A RED candle = BULLISH OB (long). A GREEN candle = BEARISH OB (short). The candle looks like the opposite trade because institutions were absorbing the last of the counter-trend orders before launching their intended direction.

At the intermediate level, OB trading is refined through candle quality filters, BOS confirmation, and multi-timeframe layering.

Refinement Techniques for Bullish OBs

  1. BOS confirmation: the OB must have been followed by a Break of Structure (break above the prior swing high). Without BOS, the move was not impulsive enough to confirm institutional participation.
  2. Body quality: the most reliable bullish OBs have large bearish bodies with small wicks. A large lower wick on the OB candle suggests indecision — narrow the entry zone to the body only (refined OB).
  3. Displacement candle: the first candle of the impulsive move after the OB should be extremely large relative to the surrounding candles. An unusually large displacement candle signals strong institutional presence.
  4. Refined OB entry: if the OB has a large lower wick, restrict the entry zone to the candle body (open to close), not the full candle range (open to low). This tightens the stop significantly.

Refinement Techniques for Bearish OBs

All the same principles apply in reverse. The bearish OB must be followed by a BOS (break below prior swing low). The candle should have a large bullish body. The displacement candle down should be unusually large. Refine the entry to the body if a large upper wick is present.

Multi-Timeframe OBs: Stacked Confluence

When a bearish OB on D1 occurs at the same price level as a bearish OB on H4, both timeframes carry institutional orders at identical levels. This is a stacked or layered OB — the most reliable form of the pattern. The D1 OB attracts price to the area; the H4 OB provides the exact entry candle.

📊 Practical Example

XAUUSD: D1 is bearish (LL/LH structure). D1 bearish OB at $2,348–$2,361 (the last bullish D1 candle before a strong drop, which also created a BOS on D1). Within the D1 OB, there is an H4 bearish OB at $2,352–$2,357. Price rallies into $2,352. H1 bearish engulfing confirms. Entry: $2,352. Stop: $2,362 (above D1 OB body high). Target: $2,290 (next D1 swing low). R:R = 1:6. Stacked D1 + H4 OBs with H1 trigger = maximum confidence.

💡 Pro Tip

Always confirm an OB entry with a lower timeframe candle signal. A bearish OB on H4 is confirmed by a bearish close or bearish engulfing candle on H1 when price touches the OB. This LTF confirmation filters out OBs that price merely passes through without reacting — saving you from entries on weak or consumed OBs.

Expert-level OB trading combines OB hierarchy, the 3-candle footprint pattern, session timing, and systematic backtesting to build a statistically validated edge.

The OB Hierarchy

Not all OBs are equal. Significance scales with timeframe:

  • D1 OBs — held for days to weeks; driven by macro institutional flow; highest significance
  • H4 OBs — held for hours to days; ideal for swing trades; high significance
  • H1 OBs — held for hours; ideal for intraday trades; moderate significance
  • M15 OBs — held for 15 minutes to 2 hours; used for entry refinement only; low standalone significance

For swing trading: focus exclusively on D1 and H4 OBs. For day trading: H1 and M15 OBs confirmed by H4 bias.

The 3-Candle OB Footprint

Advanced traders look for a specific 3-candle institutional fingerprint:

  1. Candle 1: a momentum candle in the direction of the prior trend (the last push)
  2. Candle 2: a counter-trend candle (THE ORDER BLOCK) — this is where the institution positioned itself
  3. Candle 3: a massive displacement candle that breaks the prior structure — confirming institutional execution

This 3-candle pattern is the clearest "footprint" of institutional order placement. The OB candle (Candle 2) must be visually distinct — notably different from surrounding candles in body size or position. When you see this 3-candle sequence, you are looking at a premium OB with verified institutional involvement.

Session Timing for OB Entries

The optimal entry timing for OB retests aligns with session opens:

  • A bullish OB created during New York session is ideally entered when the London session opens and price dips into it — London's institutional flow adds fresh buying to the existing OB orders
  • A bearish OB created overnight is ideally entered when the New York session opens and price rallies into it — NY institutional selling adds to existing OB orders
  • OBs tested during illiquid hours (midnight to 6am London time) produce fewer reliable reactions due to lack of institutional participation

Backtesting OBs: Building Your Edge

To validate your OB identification criteria, conduct a 100-trade backtest:

  1. Identify 100 OBs on historical charts using your current criteria
  2. Mark the entry, stop, and first target for each
  3. Record: did price react at the OB? Did it produce a profitable move to target?
  4. Calculate win rate and average R achieved

Target a reaction rate above 60%. If your rate is lower, refine one criterion at a time — add BOS requirement, add LTF confirmation, narrow to fresh OBs only — until the rate improves. This iterative process is how professional traders build a statistically validated OB edge rather than guessing.

Advanced Concepts

3-Candle Footprint — The institutional fingerprint pattern: momentum candle → OB candle → displacement candle with BOS; the highest-quality OB identification pattern.
OB Hierarchy — D1 OBs are most significant; M15 OBs least; timeframe determines how long an OB remains relevant and how strong its reactions are.
Session Timing — OBs tested at major session opens (London, New York) produce the strongest reactions due to fresh institutional participation.
📊 Practical Example

GBPUSD: 3-candle footprint on H4. Candle 1: large bullish candle pushing to a new swing high (the last upward push). Candle 2: bearish candle that retraces partially (body 1.2680–1.2694) — the BEARISH OB. Candle 3: massive bearish displacement candle breaking the prior swing low, creating BOS. D1 is bearish — structural alignment confirmed. Price rallies back to 1.2680–1.2694 at the London open. H1 bearish engulfing at 1.2682. Entry: 1.2682. Stop: 1.2698 (above OB body high). Target: 1.2570. R:R = 1:7. London session timing + D1 bearish + 3-candle OB footprint + H1 trigger = elite setup.

💡 Pro Tip

The best OB entries occur when price enters the OB during the opposing session to when the OB was created. If a bearish OB formed during the London session, the optimal short entry is when the New York session opens and price rallies into it — NY institutional order flow amplifies the reaction. Track this pattern in your journal: session of OB creation vs session of OB retest. You will find cross-session retests outperform same-session retests consistently.

Key Takeaway

Bullish OBs (last bearish candle before an up-move) give you long entries; bearish OBs (last bullish candle before a down-move) give you short entries. Refine the entry to the candle body, confirm on a lower timeframe, and always place your stop beyond the full OB (including wicks) for safety.
