Section 5 · Risk Management & Position Sizing

The 1% Rule & Position Sizing

The 1% rule is simple: never risk more than 1% of your trading account on a single trade. On a $5,000 account, 1% = $50. That is the maximum amount you can lose before you cut your loss and move on to the next setup.

This feels tiny at first. It is designed to. Trading is a long game played over hundreds of trades. Your job is to stay in the game long enough for your statistical edge to play out — and risking 1% per trade ensures you can survive a 20-trade losing streak (which is rare but possible) and still have over 80% of your account intact to fight back.

Key Terms

Risk % — The percentage of account balance you are prepared to lose on a single trade. Standard: 1%.
Dollar Risk — The actual dollar amount at risk: Account Balance × Risk%. On $5,000 at 1% = $50.
Lot Size — The number of units of an instrument traded. Standard lot = 100,000 units (forex). Mini lot = 10,000. Micro lot = 1,000.
Pip Value — The dollar value of a one-pip move for a given lot size. For EURUSD standard lot: $10 per pip.
📊 Practical Example

$5,000 account, 1% risk = $50 maximum loss. You have a EURUSD setup with a 25-pip stop loss. Pip value for a standard EURUSD lot = $10/pip. Position Size = $50 ÷ (25 pips × $10) = $50 ÷ $250 = 0.2 lots. Simple, exact, and enforced before you place the trade.

The position sizing formula converts your risk tolerance into an exact lot size every time:

The Formula

Position Size = (Account Balance × Risk%) ÷ (SL pips × Pip Value per lot)

Let's work through a XAUUSD (Gold) example with a $5,000 account:

  • Account Balance: $5,000
  • Risk per trade: 1% → Dollar risk = $50
  • Stop loss: 25 pips (on XAUUSD, 1 pip = $0.01 price movement)
  • Pip value for XAUUSD: 1 standard lot = $10/pip
  • Position Size = $50 ÷ (25 × $10) = $50 ÷ $250 = 0.2 lots

At 0.2 lots: if price moves 25 pips against you and hits your stop, you lose exactly $50 — precisely 1% of your account. The formula works for any instrument, any account size, any stop distance.

💡 Pro Tip

Pip values differ by instrument and account currency. EURUSD standard lot = $10/pip. GBPJPY can be $7–8/pip due to the JPY conversion. For exotic pairs, always check your broker's contract specifications or use their built-in position size calculator before trading — never assume the pip value.

The 1% rule is a baseline — professional risk management scales dynamically based on account performance, market regime, and correlation exposure:

Drawdown scaling: When in a 10%+ drawdown, reduce to 0.5% per trade. The reduced size lowers the hurdle for recovery mathematically — fewer large losses mean the percentage gain needed to recover stays manageable. Many professionals use a step-down schedule: 10% drawdown → 0.75% risk; 15% drawdown → 0.5%; 20% drawdown → stop trading and review.

Performance scaling: Only increase to 1.5–2% risk after demonstrating consistent profitability across 50+ trades with a positive expectancy. Never increase size during a winning streak — that is when overconfidence is most dangerous.

Advanced Concepts

Dynamic Position Sizing — Adjusting risk% based on account performance, drawdown depth, and market volatility conditions.
Portfolio Exposure — Total dollar risk across all open positions simultaneously. Should never exceed 3–5% of account balance.
Correlation Adjustment — Treating correlated positions (e.g., EURUSD + GBPUSD longs) as a single trade for total exposure calculation.
📊 Portfolio Exposure Example

$50,000 account, 1% risk = $500/trade. You have EURUSD long (SL 30 pips, $450 risk), GBPUSD long (SL 35 pips, $490 risk), and XAUUSD long (SL 40 pips, $480 risk). Total open risk = $1,420 = 2.84% — acceptable. But all three are effectively USD-short positions. A sudden USD-positive shock hits all three simultaneously. True effective risk = $1,420 on one macro event. This is why correlation checking is mandatory before adding any new position.

💡 Pro Tip

Build a simple spreadsheet with columns: Account Balance, Risk%, SL pips, Pip Value per lot, and Calculated Lot Size (auto-formula). Use it before every single trade. Also add a "Total Open Risk" cell that sums all current position dollar risks — never let this exceed 4% of your account regardless of how many trades you have open.

Key Takeaway

Position sizing is risk management made mathematical. The formula Position Size = (Account Balance × Risk%) ÷ (SL pips × Pip Value) converts your risk tolerance into a precise lot size. Calculate it for every trade, every time — no exceptions, no gut feelings.
