Section 5 · Risk Management & Position Sizing

Building a Risk Checklist

A pre-trade checklist is a written list of questions you answer before placing every single trade. It acts as a filter — trades that do not pass the checklist do not get placed. This is the primary way to prevent impulsive, emotional entries: the checklist forces a deliberate pause and a structured process before any money is at risk.

Think of it like a commercial pilot's pre-flight checklist. A pilot with 10,000 hours of experience still goes through the same checklist before every single takeoff — not because they forgot the steps, but because the checklist removes the possibility of a critical oversight caused by distraction, fatigue, or overconfidence. You should approach trading the same way.

Key Terms

Pre-Trade Checklist — A written list of criteria a trade must satisfy before you enter. Trades that fail any criterion do not get placed.
Trading Journal — A record of every trade including entry, exit, reasoning, outcome, and emotional state notes.
Session Rules — Rules governing when you trade: which market hours, which days of the week, and hard stop conditions.
📊 Simple Beginner Checklist

☐ Is this pair on my approved watchlist?
☐ Is there a clear H4 or Daily trend bias?
☐ Is my entry aligned with that bias?
☐ Have I identified a specific entry trigger?
☐ Is my stop loss at a structural level?
☐ Is my R:R at least 1:2?
☐ Have I calculated my exact position size?
☐ Is there no major news event in the next 60 minutes?

If any answer is No — do not trade.

A complete pre-trade checklist is organized into five categories, each covering a different dimension of trade quality:

Category 1 — Market Bias (HTF Analysis)
☐ Have I analyzed the Daily and H4 chart?
☐ Is the structure clearly bullish, bearish, or ranging?
☐ Am I trading with the higher-timeframe bias, not against it?

Category 2 — Entry Criteria (LTF Trigger)
☐ Is there a valid order block, demand/supply zone, or post-sweep reversal at my entry?
☐ Has the LTF shown a BOS or CHoCH in my intended direction?
☐ Did price come to my zone — or did I chase it?

Category 3 — Risk Parameters
☐ Is my SL placed at a structural invalidation point?
☐ Is my R:R at minimum 1:2?
☐ Have I calculated position size using the formula?
☐ Does adding this trade keep total portfolio risk under 4%?

Category 4 — Timing
☐ Am I in an active trading session (London or New York)?
☐ Is there a high-impact news event in the next 60 minutes? (Check ForexFactory)
☐ Is this time of day suitable for this specific instrument?

Category 5 — Psychology Check
☐ Have I already taken 2 or more losses today? (If yes — stop trading for the day)
☐ Am I entering from a valid analysis — or from FOMO?
☐ Can I lose this trade with complete emotional neutrality?

💡 Pro Tip

Read your checklist out loud before placing the trade — not silently. The physical act of speaking each question engages different neural pathways than silent reading and significantly increases the probability of catching a missed criterion. Many professional traders keep a laminated checklist card next to their monitor for this exact purpose.

Expert checklists incorporate market regime detection and macro-level context that fundamentally changes which setups are valid:

Regime detection layer: Before any other checklist items, identify the current market regime. Trending: take BOS pullbacks and OB re-tests; avoid counter-trend reversal attempts. Ranging: trade reversals only at clear structural boundaries with liquidity sweeps; avoid breakout trades until range resolves. High-volatility (news, CPI, NFP): widen stops by 50%, halve position size, or stand aside entirely — wide spreads and erratic price action make SL placement unreliable.

Advanced Checklist Template

MACRO CONTEXT
☐ Weekly bias from weekly close and D1 structure?
☐ Has a key liquidity level been swept this week?
☐ What is DXY doing? (For USD-correlated instruments)
ENTRY PRECISION
☐ Top-down confluent: D1 → H4 → H1 all aligned?
☐ LTF trigger present on M15 or M5?
☐ Entry price, SL price, and TP price written down before placing?
RISK ARCHITECTURE
☐ Total portfolio risk after this trade?
☐ Correlation check: other positions moving with same macro driver?
☐ Plan if trade is 50% toward SL? (No adjustment — let it play)
POST-TRADE DISCIPLINE
☐ No immediate re-entry for 15 minutes win or lose.
☐ Journal entry completed before closing platform.
📊 Correlation Check Example

You have XAUUSD long, EURUSD long, and AUDUSD long open simultaneously. All three are effectively betting on USD weakness. If the Fed issues an unexpected hawkish statement, all three hit their SLs at the same time — you have 3× risk concentrated on one macro event. Your checklist's correlation check would have flagged this: only one USD-correlated position at a time, or split the 1% risk budget across all of them so total exposure remains 1%.

💡 Pro Tip

Review your checklist compliance monthly. Were there trades where you skipped items? Trades where you overrode a "No" answer? Treat checklist violations with the same seriousness as trade losses — the violation is often the real source of the problem. A trader who follows their checklist 100% of the time and takes a loss is demonstrating discipline. A trader who skips the checklist and wins is building a dangerous habit.

Key Takeaway

A pre-trade checklist is your system for making rational decisions in an environment designed to trigger irrational ones. Build it once from the five categories, use it for every trade without exception, and treat violations as seriously as losses. The checklist is not optional — it is the foundation of consistent execution.
