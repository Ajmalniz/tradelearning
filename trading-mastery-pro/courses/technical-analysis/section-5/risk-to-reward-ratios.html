Section 5 · Risk Management & Position Sizing

Risk-to-Reward Ratios

The Risk-to-Reward ratio (R:R) measures how much you stand to gain versus how much you risk on a single trade. A 1:2 R:R means: risk $50 to potentially make $100. A 1:3 means: risk $50 to potentially make $150.

The power of R:R becomes clear with simple math: at a 1:2 R:R, you only need to win 34% of your trades to break even. Win 40% and you are profitable. You can be wrong more often than you are right and still grow your account — which is a realistic target, since even experienced traders typically win only 40–55% of trades.

Key Terms

R:R Ratio — The ratio of potential profit (reward) to potential loss (risk) on a single trade.
Take Profit (TP) — The price level at which you close your trade to realize a profit.
Break-Even Win Rate — The minimum percentage of winning trades needed to avoid losing money at a given R:R.
1R — Shorthand for the amount you risked on a trade. A 2R win = profit of twice your risked amount.
📊 Break-Even Win Rate by R:R

At 1:1 R:R → need 50% win rate to break even.
At 1:2 R:R → need 34% win rate to break even.
At 1:3 R:R → need 25% win rate to break even.

Example at 40% win rate with 1:2 R:R — over 10 trades, risking $50 each: 4 winners × $100 = $400 profit; 6 losers × $50 = $300 loss. Net: +$100 despite losing 60% of trades.

Setting realistic take profit targets uses structural levels — the next significant resistance (for longs) or support (for shorts) where opposing pressure is likely to push back. Your TP is not an arbitrary multiple; it is the nearest structural obstacle in your trade's path.

The process for setting TP: (1) Identify your entry and stop loss — this defines your risk and 1R. (2) Measure the pip/dollar distance from entry to SL — this is 1R. (3) Locate the nearest structural resistance (for longs) on your timeframe. (4) Calculate how many R's that level is from your entry. (5) If the level is only 0.8R away — skip the trade. The R:R does not justify the risk. (6) If it is 2R or more with no structural resistance blocking the path — the trade meets minimum criteria.

Minimum standard: 1:2 R:R on every trade, no exceptions. One trade with 1:3 R:R compensates for two full losses — this is the asymmetric edge that makes trading profitable over time.

💡 Pro Tip

Place your TP just before the opposing structural zone — not inside it. If there is a supply zone at 1.2050–1.2080 on EURUSD and you are in a long, set TP at 1.2045, not 1.2065. Price rarely reaches the center of a supply zone cleanly; capturing 85% of the available move is better than getting greedy and having price reverse at the zone edge and take back all of your profit.

Asymmetric position management extends the R:R concept beyond binary win/loss outcomes. Rather than closing the entire position at one target, scaling out improves average realized R on winners while keeping you in high-conviction trades for larger moves.

Partial profit approach: Close 50% of the position at 1:1 or 1:1.5 R:R to lock in profit and move the SL to breakeven on the remainder. This removes risk from the trade while giving the remaining position a free run at the larger target. Your worst case on the full position is now 0 (breakeven) instead of -1R.

Scale-out approach: Close 1/3 at 1:1, 1/3 at 1:2, 1/3 at 1:3 or trailing stop. Average realized R on a full winner = approximately 2R. A full loser = -1R. Expected value on this model with 40% win rate: (0.40 × 2.0) − (0.60 × 1.0) = 0.80 − 0.60 = +0.20R per trade.

Advanced Concepts

Expected Value (EV) — Average profit per trade: (Win Rate × Avg Win) − (Loss Rate × Avg Loss). Positive EV = profitable system.
Partial Close — Closing a portion of your position at an intermediate target, locking in profit while leaving the rest running.
Scale Out — Progressively closing portions of a trade at multiple targets as it moves in your favor.
📊 Expected Value — Full Calculation

$10,000 account, 1% risk = $100/trade, 50 trades per month. Win rate = 42%, average realized R:R = 2.2 (scale-out method), average loss = 1R. EV per trade = (0.42 × 2.2) − (0.58 × 1.0) = 0.924 − 0.580 = +0.344R. Monthly profit = 50 × 0.344 × $100 = $1,720. That is 17.2% monthly from a sub-50% win rate using disciplined R:R management — no special setups required.

💡 Pro Tip

Record every trade in your journal: planned R:R vs. actual R:R achieved (not planned). After 100 trades, you will see your true average R:R — most traders discover it is 30–40% lower than planned because they exit early out of fear. The data changes your behavior in a way that willpower alone cannot.

Key Takeaway

R:R is the backbone of long-run trading profitability. A 1:2 minimum ratio means you can lose more trades than you win and still grow your account. Set TP at structural levels, enforce the minimum standard on every single trade, and never close a winning trade early out of fear.
