Section 1 · Course Introduction

How to Use This Course

Welcome to Technical Analysis Mastery. This course is designed to take you from zero knowledge to a complete, professional-grade trading system — one concept at a time.

Follow the sections in order. Each section builds directly on the previous one. Section 2 (Market Structure) makes no sense without Section 1's foundation. Section 3 (Supply & Demand) requires you to understand structure from Section 2. Don't skip ahead.

Every lesson has three depth levels — Beginner, Intermediate, and Expert. Start at Beginner. When you feel confident with the beginner content, move to Intermediate. Only read the Expert tab when the concept feels solid. There's no rush — depth beats speed.

Key Terms

Demo Account — A simulated trading account with virtual money, used for practice without financial risk.
Top-Down Analysis — Starting analysis on higher timeframes (Weekly, Daily) before drilling to lower timeframes (H4, H1) for entry.
📊 Practical Example

After completing Section 2 (Market Structure), open TradingView and find a currency pair you like. Before moving to Section 3, spend 30 minutes identifying HH, HL, LH, and LL on the H4 chart. Mark the BOS and CHoCH if you can find them. Only when you can do this confidently should you move to the next section.

The fastest path to becoming a skilled trader is deliberate practice, not passive reading. For every concept you read in this course, you should spend at least as much time applying it on a chart before moving on.

Here's the recommended study loop for each lesson:

  1. Read the Beginner level fully and take notes in your own words.
  2. Open a chart and find 3 examples of the concept you just learned.
  3. Read the Intermediate level and upgrade your understanding.
  4. Mark Complete only when you can explain the concept to someone else without notes.
  5. Read Expert for context, but don't pressure yourself to apply it immediately.

Use the sidebar search to revisit lessons when you encounter a concept in a later section that you don't fully remember. The course is designed to be a reference, not just a one-time read.

💡 Pro Tip

Keep a trading journal from Day 1, even before you place a single trade. Write down your chart observations, the patterns you spot, and what you expect to happen. When you revisit later, you'll see how your analytical eye improves. This journal becomes your most valuable asset.

By the time you reach this level, you should be approaching the course as a system builder. The goal of Section 6 is to synthesize everything into a repeatable, rules-based trading process with clearly defined entry conditions, risk parameters, and trade management rules.

Professional traders are successful because they have a process, not because they have insight. Your edge comes from consistency — executing the same well-defined setup over hundreds of trades. The course gives you the building blocks; your job is to assemble them into a system that fits your schedule, risk tolerance, and personality.

Critical mindset shift at the expert level: detach from individual trade outcomes. Any single trade can be a loser even with a perfect setup. Judge your trading by the quality of your process over 30+ trades, not by whether today's trade made money.

💡 Pro Tip

After completing the entire course, go back and take the Final Assessment with honest rigor. Any question you get wrong should send you back to the corresponding lesson. True mastery means 90%+ on the assessment — don't move to live trading until you're there.

Key Takeaway

Follow the sections in order, practice every concept on a real chart before marking it complete, and build toward a systematic trading process. The course is a reference you'll return to — not a one-time read.
