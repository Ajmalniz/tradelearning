Section 1 · Course Introduction

Tools and Platforms

TradingView is the industry-standard charting platform and the only tool you need to complete this course. It runs in any web browser and has a free tier that gives you access to real-time charts for Forex, Crypto, Stocks, and Commodities.

To get started: go to TradingView, create a free account, and open a chart. In the search bar at the top, type "XAUUSD" (Gold vs US Dollar). Set the timeframe to H4 using the dropdown at the top. This is your primary workspace for this course.

The key chart elements to recognize: the green (bullish) and red (bearish) candlesticks, the price axis on the right, and the time axis at the bottom. Everything else is optional for now.

Key Terms

TradingView — A web-based charting platform used by millions of retail and institutional traders worldwide.
Candlestick Chart — A chart type showing OHLC (Open, High, Low, Close) data as colored candle bodies with wicks.
H4 / H1 / M15 — Chart timeframes: H4 = 4 hours per candle, H1 = 1 hour, M15 = 15 minutes.
📊 Practical Example

Open TradingView → search "EURUSD" → set to H4 timeframe. You'll see a series of green and red candles. Each candle represents 4 hours of trading. The top wick shows the highest price reached, the bottom wick shows the lowest, and the body shows where price opened and closed within those 4 hours.

For live or demo trading, you'll need a broker and a trading platform. MetaTrader 5 (MT5) is the most widely used retail trading platform. Download it free from your broker's website. MT5 offers more order types and a built-in strategy tester for backtesting.

Key indicators to add to your TradingView charts (keep them minimal):

  • EMA 20 / EMA 50 / EMA 200 — Exponential Moving Averages for trend bias confirmation
  • ATR (Average True Range) — Measures volatility; critical for setting stop loss distance
  • Volume — Confirms the strength of breakouts and reversals

Use TradingView's drawing tools (horizontal lines, rectangles, trend lines) to mark your supply/demand zones, order blocks, and liquidity levels. Save your chart layouts — the paid "layouts" feature is worth the investment once you're actively trading.

💡 Pro Tip

Keep your charts clean. Beginners add 10+ indicators; professionals use 2-3. Price action and a few key moving averages tell you everything you need. Cluttered charts lead to decision paralysis.

At the professional level, your platform setup becomes a competitive tool. Multi-monitor setups allow you to monitor multiple timeframes simultaneously — for example: Monitor 1 = Daily/Weekly for structure, Monitor 2 = H4 for setup, Monitor 3 = H1/M15 for entry timing.

TradingView Pro features worth the upgrade: custom alerts (price crossing a level sends a phone notification), multiple chart layouts, and no ad interruptions. For serious traders, TradingView Premium adds more saved indicators and priority support.

For broker selection, evaluate: spread (transaction cost per trade), execution speed (slippage), regulation (use FCA, ASIC, CySEC-regulated brokers only), and the instruments offered. Low spreads matter more for scalpers; for swing traders, execution quality matters more than the 0.1-pip spread difference.

Pine Script (TradingView's built-in scripting language) allows you to code custom indicators and backtests. Even basic Pine Script knowledge lets you automate level detection and alert triggering — a significant edge for active traders.

Key Terms

Spread — The difference between the bid (sell) and ask (buy) price; the broker's primary transaction fee.
Slippage — The difference between your intended entry price and the actual fill price, caused by fast-moving markets.
Pine Script — TradingView's programming language for creating custom indicators and strategy backtests.
💡 Pro Tip

Never trade live without first testing your strategy on a demo account for at least 30 trades. Demo trading teaches platform mechanics — order types, position sizing, stop loss placement — without the emotional pressure of real money on the line.

Key Takeaway

TradingView (free) is all you need to complete this course. Keep your charts clean — price action is your primary tool. Add MT5 for live trading only after you've proven your strategy on demo for a minimum of 30 trades.
