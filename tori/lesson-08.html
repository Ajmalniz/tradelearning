All Setups, Same Strategy

Every setup you'll learn is built on the same foundation: trend lines, top-down analysis, action lines, safety lines. What changes is when and how price interacts with your lines. Learning these variations helps you recognize and categorize different market behaviors — and eventually track which ones work best for you.

"These are just different types of setups. All the same strategy — just different ways to look at it."

Setup 1: Break & Retest

The break and retest is a specific sequence of price behavior after breaking a trend line:

  1. Price breaks the action line
  2. Price then comes back and touches the broken line from the other side
  3. After the retest, price continues in the breakout direction
🔄

What the Retest Looks Like

Imagine a downward trend line holding price down. Price finally breaks above it — that's the break. Then price pulls back down and touches that same line from above before going higher. That pullback and touch from the other side is the retest. The old resistance becomes new support.

🔵 Break & Retest

  1. Draw trend lines with top-down analysis
  2. Wait for price to break your action line
  3. Watch for price to pull back and touch the broken line from the other side
  4. That retest touch is your entry signal
  5. Safety line is the opposing trend line
  6. Exit when safety line breaks

🟢 Bounce Setup

  1. Draw trend lines with top-down analysis
  2. Wait for price to touch your trend line (don't wait for a break)
  3. Enter when price touches the line, anticipating it will bounce
  4. The touched line IS your action AND safety line
  5. Stay in as long as price bounces
  6. Exit when price finally breaks through the line

Setup 2: The Bounce Playbook

The bounce is a fundamentally different approach compared to the trend line break. Instead of waiting for price to break the line, you're anticipating that price will bounce off the line.

⬆️

Entering at the Touch, Not the Break

In a bounce setup, when you see price approaching your trend line, you don't wait for it to cross. Instead, you enter as price touches the line, expecting it to bounce away. This is an earlier entry — but it comes with a different risk profile.

The Special Rule for Bounce Setups

In a bounce setup, the action line and the safety line are the same line. Here's why:

  • You enter because price touched your trend line (expecting it to bounce)
  • You stay in as long as price keeps bouncing off that same line
  • You exit when price finally breaks through that same line — that's when your "safety" fails
⚠️

Early Entry, Higher Uncertainty

The bounce setup was something Tori implemented in her early trading years. It's more aggressive — you're entering before the break, which means you're betting on the line holding. The trend line break setups (2-touch, 3-touch, break & retest) all enter AFTER confirmation. The bounce enters before. Both are valid — just different risk profiles.

How to Choose Your Setup

Don't try to trade all of them at once. Start with the core trend line break. Get comfortable with 2-touch and 3-touch variations. Then experiment with the break & retest and bounce when you're ready. Track each separately in your journal and let the data tell you which setups suit your instrument and trading style.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 8

  • Break & Retest: price breaks the line, then pulls back to touch it from the other side → enter.
  • Bounce: enter when price TOUCHES a trend line (before it breaks), expecting a bounce.
  • In bounce setups, action line and safety line are the SAME line.
  • Bounce entry is earlier = higher uncertainty, different risk profile.
  • All setups: same system, different entry timing and triggers.
  • The core trend line break is still the "meat" — other setups are variations.
  • Track each setup type separately to find what works best for you.
