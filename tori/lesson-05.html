What Makes a Setup Low Risk or High Risk?

Before you place any trade, you need to assess one critical factor: how far is the current price from the safety line? This single visual measurement tells you whether the setup is low risk or high risk.

"A low-risk setup is a trade opportunity where the potential loss is small and clearly defined while the potential gain could be significantly larger. Think of it like a 'why not?' trade — if you're just going to put cents on this and you could potentially make dollars, it's a low-risk trade."

The Visual Measurement

You don't need a calculator for this. You need your eyes. After price breaks the action line, look at the chart and ask: how far is the current price from the safety line?

  • Small distance to safety line = Low risk setup ✓
  • Large distance to safety line = High risk setup ✗

The distance matters because it represents how much money you'd lose if the trade goes wrong and hits your stop-loss.

✅ Low Risk Setup

  • Price is CLOSE to the safety line
  • Potential loss is small if wrong
  • Potential gain could be much larger
  • Favorable risk/reward ratio
  • The "why not?" trade
  • This is what you're looking for

⚠️ High Risk Setup

  • Price is FAR from the safety line
  • Potential loss is large if wrong
  • Risk/reward is unfavorable
  • This setup is NOT worth taking
  • Even if the direction is right, skip it
  • Wait for a better entry

The "Why Not?" Trade

💡 The "Why Not?" Mindset

When you find a low-risk setup, ask yourself: "If my loss is just a few dollars and my potential gain is much larger — why NOT take this trade?" This mindset keeps you focused on finding setups where the math works overwhelmingly in your favor. You're not gambling; you're engineering a situation where winning costs nothing and losing costs almost nothing too.

When Will You Learn the Numbers?

Right now, we're identifying risk visually. In the next lesson, we'll add exact numbers — percentages, dollar amounts, specific stop-loss placement. But the visual identification comes first because you need to be able to look at a setup and instantly know whether it's worth taking without doing math every time.

👁️

Train Your Eyes First

Experienced traders can glance at a chart and immediately know if a setup is low or high risk. That visual skill starts here. Eventually, the numbers and percentages confirm what your eyes already saw.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 5

  • Low risk = price is CLOSE to the safety line when the action line breaks.
  • High risk = price is FAR from the safety line when the action line breaks.
  • The safety line distance = your maximum potential loss if wrong.
  • Always visually assess risk before entering any trade.
  • The "why not?" trade: small potential loss, large potential gain.
  • A high-risk setup is worth skipping even if you're confident in the direction.
