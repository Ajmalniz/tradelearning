Why Journaling Is Non-Negotiable

You've learned the system. You're practicing on sim charts. Now comes the most boring-sounding — and most important — habit in all of trading: journaling every single trade.

"Journaling helps you see reoccurring mistakes or reoccurring setups that are working or not. This is where you get a bird's eye view. We need to track this — to see what's working and what's not."
🔍

Spots Patterns

See which setup types are making you money and which aren't. Data doesn't lie.

🚫

Stops Impulse Trades

When every trade is recorded, you think twice before taking a random one.

🧯

Prevents Revenge Trading

A bad trade on record makes you think before blindly trying to "get it back."

📈

Shows Progress

Looking back at your first trade vs. your hundredth is profoundly motivating.

⬆️

Justifies Scaling Up

Data showing consistent profitability is the ONLY reason to increase position size.

🔬

Powers the Experiment

Without tracking, you can't run the science experiment. You're just guessing.

What to Track on Every Trade

Minimum Information Per Trade

Date & time of entry
Instrument (e.g., Crude Oil)
Setup type (2-touch, retest, bounce)
Entry price
Stop-loss level
Exit price
P&L (profit or loss amount)
Win or loss
Notes / reasoning
Emotional state / mistakes

Journaling Tools — Your Options

📓 Notebook (Manual)

Write down every trade by hand. The original method — still works.

✓ No tech required, full control
△ Hard to analyze data in aggregate

📊 Spreadsheet (Excel/Google)

Type your trades into a spreadsheet. Enables formulas and charts.

✓ Easy to total and filter data
△ Still manual entry, can be tedious

🖼️ Screenshots

Screenshot the chart at entry and exit. Capture the visual setup.

✓ Visual record of every trade
△ Hard to analyze data across trades

🎥 Voice/Video Logs

Record yourself talking through the trade reasoning and outcome.

✓ Captures your emotional state
△ Time-consuming to review

⚡ Tradezella

A dedicated trading journal platform that syncs with your brokerage automatically.

✓ Auto-imports trades, tags setup types, shows analytics
△ Subscription required

📱 Platform Journal

Some brokers/platforms have built-in journaling tools.

✓ Integrated with your trades
△ Varies widely by platform quality

The Mantras of Journaling

Track → Journal → Trade → Repeat

"Track, journal, trade. Track, journal, trade. Every single trade: the setup, the reasoning you took the trade, the outcome. This becomes part of your experiment."

When to Increase Position Size

This is the payoff of journaling. After weeks and months of tracking, your data will either confirm that you're profitable — or it won't. You should never increase your position size based on a feeling. Increase only when your data shows:

  • Consistent profitability over enough trades to be statistically meaningful
  • Your winners outweigh your losers
  • A specific setup type you're confident in
  • Emotional discipline (low impulsive trades in your journal)
📊

Data Justifies Confidence

Moving from 1 micro contract to 2, or from $3,000 risk to $6,000, should never be based on "I feel ready." It should be based on: your journal shows X weeks of profitability, your win rate is Y%, your average winner is Z times your average loser. That's a justified decision. Everything else is gambling.
