What Is a Trend Line &
How to Draw It Correctly

The single tool that powers the entire strategy — and the three rules you must never break when drawing one.

⏱ 10 min 🟢 Beginner 📈 Foundation

The Holy Grail Is a Line

When traders think about complex systems, they imagine indicators, algorithms, and multi-screen setups. Tori's strategy strips all of that away. The entire system — every entry, every exit, every risk decision — comes down to one tool:

"This right here is the holy grail. This is the system. This is everything. It is the trend line."

A trend line is a simple tool that helps you see the direction of price visually. It's not magic. It's a line on a chart. But when drawn correctly and used with discipline, it tells you when to get in, when to stay, and when to get out.

The Ray Tool in TradingView

The tool used to draw trend lines in TradingView is officially called the Ray Tool. You'll find it in the drawing toolbar on the left side of the screen. Despite the technical name, we call it simply a trend line.

💡

Two Clicks, One Line

Click once to set Point A (your first touch point — the pivot). Click again to set Point B (your second touch point — this determines the angle). Once Point B is placed, the line is locked in on the chart.

The Three Unbreakable Rules

Every trend line you draw must follow all three of these rules simultaneously. If any one is violated, the line is wrong — delete it and redraw.

1

Correct Angle

Downward trend lines must angle DOWN. Upward trend lines must angle UP. No horizontal lines count as trend lines.

2

Maximum Touch Points

Try to capture as many touch points as possible. More touches = stronger line. Every touch confirms the trend line is "real."

3

No Intersection

Price CANNOT cross through, poke through, or intersect the line. This is the most critical rule. If price cuts through — the line is invalid.

Downward vs Upward Trend Lines

There are exactly two types of trend lines in this system:

Point A and Point B — The Building Blocks

📍

Point A — The Pivot

Point A is your first click — the starting touch point. It stays fixed. Think of it as the anchor. This is usually the most significant high or low you can see on the screen.

📐

Point B — The Angle Setter

Point B is your second click — this determines the angle of the entire line. Drag it to the most recent touch point to capture as many touches as possible without price intersecting.

Pro tip: Always drag Point B to the MOST RECENT touch point. This keeps the touch point visible on screen so you don't lose your reference when zooming in to lower time frames.

The Most Common Mistake

The single most common mistake when drawing trend lines is allowing price to cross through the line. This feels tempting because you want to "capture" a touch point, but it invalidates the entire line.

❌

Wrong: Price Cuts Through

If any candle (including its wick) crosses to the other side of your trend line, the line is incorrect. Take a screenshot of what this looks like and label it "wrong, wrong, wrong" — never draw a line that allows intersection.

✅

Right: Price Respects the Line

A correct trend line has price touching it (ideally multiple times) but never crossing to the other side. The line truly "holds" the price in place — either holding it up (upward) or pushing it down (downward).

Wicks vs Bodies — Tori's Approach

In Tori's system, wicks are included when drawing trend lines during top-down analysis. This is a personal rule that makes the system more precise and repeatable. When you see a wick touching or approaching your trend line, that counts as a touch point.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 1

  • A trend line is the entire strategy — everything flows from this single tool.
  • The TradingView tool is called the "Ray Tool" — we call it a trend line.
  • Rule 1: Correct angle (down = downward, up = upward).
  • Rule 2: Capture as many touch points as possible.
  • Rule 3: Price CANNOT intersect or cross through — this is the most critical rule.
  • Point A = first touch (pivot, stays fixed). Point B = second touch (sets the angle).
  • Downward = red, holds price DOWN. Upward = green, holds price UP.
  • Always include wicks when drawing lines in this system.
