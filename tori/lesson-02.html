Top Down Analysis —
Bird's Eye to Ground Level

How to structure your chart analysis starting at the monthly view and working down — and why every line must connect to the last.

⏱ 14 min 🟢 Beginner 🔍 Analysis

What Is Top Down Analysis?

Top down analysis means starting from the highest time frame and working your way down to lower and lower time frames. It's exactly what it sounds like — you start at the top (big picture) and move down (zooming in closer and closer).

"Top down analysis means we are starting from the highest time frame and working our way down. A bird's eye view first — what has this instrument done overall? Then slowly coming in closer and closer."

Think of it like using Google Maps. You start by viewing the entire country, then the state, then the city, then the street. Each zoom level gives you more detail — but you always need that higher-level context first.

The Dinosaur Icon

When you select the monthly time frame in TradingView, you'll see a dinosaur icon on the chart. This means you're viewing the very beginning of the instrument's price history — the true bird's eye view of everything it has done from day one to today.

How to Draw on Each Time Frame

The process is the same on every single time frame — just rinse and repeat:

  1. 1

    Find the highest point you see on screen

    This becomes your starting reference for drawing a downward trend line (Point A).

  2. 2

    Draw your downward trend line (red)

    From the highest point, capture as many touch points as possible. Price cannot intersect. Angle must be downward.

  3. 3

    Draw your upward trend line (green)

    From the lowest point you see, hold the price up. Capture touch points. No intersection. Angle must be upward.

  4. 4

    Move to the next lower time frame

    Adjust your existing lines slightly if needed (zooming in reveals more precision), then draw additional steeper lines.

  5. 5

    Repeat until you reach your trading time frame

    Each time frame adds more lines, more structure, more precision. Keep going until you reach your designated chart.

The Connection Rule — The Most Important Part

Every Line Must Connect to the Previous One

The most recent touch point (Point B) of any trend line becomes the starting point (Point A) of the next trend line drawn at a lower time frame. All lines must touch each other. Every single one.

This isn't optional — it's the structure that makes the top-down analysis systematic and repeatable. When you zoom in from monthly to weekly, the last touch point of your monthly trend line is exactly where your weekly trend line begins.

🔗

For Beginners: "All Lines Must Touch Each Other"

Think of it like a chain. Each link connects to the next. Every trend line you draw connects to the previous one through the shared touch point. If a line doesn't connect — something is wrong with your analysis.

Adjusting Lines as You Zoom In

When you move from the monthly to the weekly chart, your existing lines may look slightly off. This is completely normal — you're getting more precise. Make small adjustments to keep the lines accurate, but don't completely redraw them. The direction and key touch points should remain roughly the same.

⚠️

Slight Adjustments vs Full Redraws

On higher time frames, small imprecisions are acceptable. As you zoom into lower time frames, you refine your lines to be more exact. The goal is precision — but not to the point of losing your original structure.

Why Top Down Analysis Works

Price at every time frame is influenced by the higher time frames above it. A major downward trend line on the monthly chart affects how price behaves on the daily. By analyzing from the top down, you're ensuring your trades are aligned with the bigger picture.

When you arrive at your trading time frame with all your lines drawn, you have a complete map of price structure. You know where price came from, how it has behaved at every level, and exactly where you're waiting for it to break.

🗺️

A Complete Map Before You Trade

Most traders jump straight to their trading time frame and draw lines without context. Top-down analysis gives you the full picture first. By the time you arrive at the 5-minute chart, you have weeks, months, or even years of price structure to guide you.
