Phase 2 · Lesson 4

Action Lines & Safety Lines
— When Lines Get Names

Before price breaks, you have two unnamed lines. After price breaks, both lines get names — and everything changes.

⏱ 10 min🟢 Beginner🏷️ Naming

Lines Without Names

After completing your top-down analysis, your chart has two trend lines on it — one downward, one upward. At this stage, they're just lines. They don't have names. You're simply waiting for price to act.

"At first it was just simply an upward line and a downward line. We don't have an action and a safety until price breaks."

This is intentional. You don't label the lines ahead of time because you genuinely don't know which one price will break. Labeling them prematurely would mean guessing — and this strategy doesn't guess.

The Moment Everything Changes

When price finally breaks one of your trend lines, two things happen simultaneously:

  1. The broken line earns the name Action Line
  2. The other line earns the name Safety Line

Before the break: just an upward and a downward trend line. After the break: an action line and a safety line. The same two lines — completely transformed by what price just did.

Before the Break
↘ Downward Trend Line
↗ Upward Trend Line
After Price Breaks a Line
⚡ Action Line (broken)
🛡️ Safety Line (opposite)

The Action Line — Your Entry Trigger

⚡

Action Line

The action line is the trend line that price broke. Breaking this line is the trigger — the moment you enter the trade. The word "action" is perfect: it means this line prompted you to take action. Once it's broken, your trade begins.

Remember: the action line only gets named after it's been broken. While it's intact, it's just a trend line. It becomes an action line the moment price passes through it.

The Safety Line — Your Everything

🛡️

Safety Line

The safety line is the trend line that was NOT broken — the one on the opposite side. This line now serves as your guide for how long to stay in the trade, where to place your stop-loss, and when to exit. It keeps you safe. It is everything.

⚡ Action Line

  • The line that was broken
  • Triggers your entry
  • Named AFTER the break
  • Direction tells you the trade direction

🛡️ Safety Line

  • The line that was NOT broken
  • Guides your stay-in decision
  • Equals your stop-loss zone
  • Breaking it = time to exit

What Direction Is Your Trade?

The action line tells you which direction to trade:

📊

Reading the Direction

Downward trend line breaks (bullish): Price broke above the downward trend line → enter a LONG (buy) trade.

Upward trend line breaks (bearish): Price broke below the upward trend line → enter a SHORT (sell) trade.

You never decide the direction yourself. Price decides. When it breaks a line, it's telling you exactly which way it wants to go — and you follow.

The Safety Line Is Inviolable

While you're in a trade, the safety line must be respected at all times. As long as price continues to respect (bounce off) the safety line, you stay in the trade — even if the trade is temporarily moving against you.

Safety line equals risk management. Safety line equals stop-loss. Safety line equals risk. Whatever term you prefer — it is the safety line that tells you everything.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 4

  • Before price breaks: just upward and downward lines. No names yet.
  • After price breaks ONE line: both lines get named simultaneously.
  • Action line = the broken line → triggered your entry.
  • Safety line = the opposite line → tells you how long to stay in.
  • Safety line = your stop-loss zone = your risk management.
  • Direction: downward breaks → long; upward breaks → short.
  • You exit when price breaks the safety line. Not before, not after.
