Everything Comes Together Here

This is it. The final lesson walks through a complete live trade on crude oil — from opening a blank chart to closing the position. Every concept from every previous lesson appears in this walkthrough. This is the strategy in action.

Step 1 — Start at the Monthly (The Dinosaur View)

Open crude oil on TradingView. Navigate to the monthly time frame. You should see the dinosaur icon, meaning you're looking at the entire history of this instrument.

Monthly — Point A and Point B

Find the highest point on the monthly chart. That's Point A of your first downward trend line. Drag Point B to the most recent touch point, capturing as many touches as possible without price intersecting. Change the color to red. Then draw your upward trend line (green) from the lowest point visible, same rules. You now have your first two lines.

The Full Walkthrough — Step by Step

1

Monthly → Weekly: Connect and Adjust

Move from monthly to weekly. Your existing lines may look slightly off — make slight adjustments. The most recent Point B of your monthly lines becomes the starting Point A for any new weekly lines. When you zoom in, you may find you don't need many new lines if the existing ones still hold.

2

Weekly → Daily: Add Structure

Move to the daily time frame. Now you can draw additional trend lines with steeper angles as price action becomes more visible. Every new line's Point A connects to the previous line's Point B. By the daily chart, your analysis is getting more precise and you're seeing more price structure.

3

Daily → 4 Hour → 1 Hour

Continue working down. Each time frame requires slight adjustments to existing lines and drawing new, steeper lines. At the 4-hour and 1-hour charts, use TradingView's zoom button to see price more clearly. Your lines are getting more precise with each step down.

4

1 Hour → 30 Min → 5 Min

Final descent. Move to the 5-minute chart. This is where the most information lives — and the most "noise." Make your final adjustments. Draw the steepest trend lines yet, capturing the most recent price action at this granular level. Always drag Point B to the most recent touch.

5

Arrive at the 5-Minute: All Lines Connect

You now have a complete top-down analysis. Every line connects to every other line. Price has not intersected any of them. You have a clear picture of the market structure from the bird's eye monthly view all the way down to the 5-minute ground level.

6

Wait for Price to Approach a Line

Watch the 5-minute chart. If price is not near any of your lines — that's your cue to draw steeper, shorter-term trend lines to bring the action closer. Continue adding lines until price is approaching one. Then watch and wait. The moment you've been training for is coming.

7

The Break — Price Breaks the Downward Trend Line

Price breaks upward through the downward trend line. This is the action signal. The downward trend line is now the action line. The upward trend line on the other side is now the safety line. This is a LONG trade — price just told us it wants to go up.

8

Assess: Is This Low Risk or High Risk?

Before entering, look at the distance from the current price to the safety line (the upward trend line). Is it small or large? For the paper account, we're working with $100,000, so our fee at 2% would be $2,000 — but even $100 (0.1%) is a valid fee to practice with.

9

Open the Trading Panel and Enter Long

Open the paper trading panel at the bottom of TradingView. Place a long (buy) position. The TP (take profit) is optional — skip it. The SL (stop-loss) is mandatory. Drag the stop-loss to the OTHER side of the safety line — just beyond it. The panel will show you your dollar risk. Confirm it matches your fee.

10

The Ride — Watch the Safety Line

You're in the trade. Price is moving (hopefully upward). Watch the safety line. As long as price respects it — bouncing off or staying above — you stay in. If the price goes to $90 above your entry, you're up $90. $120 above — up $120. The safety line is the only thing you're watching.

11

The Exit — Safety Line Breaks

When price breaks the safety line, the trade is done. Close your position immediately. Calculate your P&L. Log it in your journal with the setup type, your reasoning, and the outcome. This is a complete round trip trade: entry → management → exit.

And Tomorrow?

Come back tomorrow. Open the chart. Do your top-down analysis again — because markets have moved, lines need updating, and new structure has formed. Rinse and repeat. This is the daily practice of a trend line trader.

The Cycle Never Stops

Mark up → wait → act → manage → exit → journal → repeat. Every session. The more reps you get, the faster and more confident you become. There's no shortcut — the reps ARE the shortcut.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 11

  • Start every trade with full top-down analysis — monthly all the way to 5-minute.
  • Every line connects to the previous; all rules apply at every time frame.
  • Wait for the break on the 5-minute chart. Don't force it.
  • Downward line breaks → long trade. Upward line breaks → short trade.
  • Stop-loss: drag to the OTHER side of the safety line. Never on the safety line.
  • Stay in while safety line holds. Exit the moment it breaks.
  • Log every trade. Come back tomorrow. Repeat the top-down analysis. Get your reps.

Final Knowledge Check

Test your understanding of the complete system

1. What time frame do you START your top-down analysis on?

A) 1-hour — that's the most common trading time frame
B) Daily — where most swing traders operate
C) Monthly — the highest bird's eye view of the instrument's history
D) 5-minute — where you'll be placing trades
✅ Correct! Always start at the monthly. Top DOWN means you begin at the top (highest time frame) and work your way down to your trading time frame.

2. In the crude oil trade walkthrough, price broke the DOWNWARD trend line. What position was entered?

A) LONG (buy) — price breaking upward above the downward line signals bullish momentum
B) SHORT (sell) — price breaking the downward line confirms the downtrend
C) No position — wait for the upward line to break too
D) LONG and SHORT simultaneously as a hedge
✅ Correct! When price breaks upward through the downward trend line, it's a bullish signal → enter a LONG (buy) position. The action line direction tells you the trade direction.

3. Where exactly was the stop-loss placed in the walkthrough?

A) On the safety line (the upward trend line)
B) On the action line (the broken downward trend line)
C) On the OTHER SIDE of the safety line — beyond it, giving price breathing room
D) At a fixed distance of 50 pips below entry
✅ Correct! Stop-loss goes on the other side of the safety line, not ON it. This gives price room to test the safety line without immediately stopping you out.

4. You're in the crude oil trade. Price has moved up nicely and is respecting your safety line. What do you do?

A) Close the trade now to lock in the profit
B) Move your stop-loss to break even immediately
C) Stay in the trade — as long as price respects the safety line, you hold
D) Add to your position since it's going well
✅ Correct! You stay in as long as price respects the safety line. The safety line — not your emotions, not a target, not a timer — tells you when to exit. Stay until it breaks.
