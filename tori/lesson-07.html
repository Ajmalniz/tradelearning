The Trend Line Break —
2-Touch & 3-Touch Point Setups

The meat of the strategy. Learn the science experiment approach: how many times did price respect the line before breaking?

⏱ 12 min🟡 Intermediate🔬 Setups

Getting More Scientific

You now understand the core strategy: draw lines, wait for the break, enter, exit when the safety line breaks. That's the foundation. Now we get more scientific about it. We start asking a specific question about every setup we encounter:

"When price breaks and I get an action, how many touch points, how many times has the price respected these lines? This is called a trend line break — but how many touches did it have?"

This is where you shift from understanding the system to experimenting with it. You treat each trade as data — and different types of setups have different characteristics you can track and optimize over time.

The Basic Trend Line Break

The simplest version of the setup is the trend line break: price breaks one of your trend lines, you enter, the other becomes your safety line. This is the foundational setup — every other variation builds on it.

📊

No Indicators Required

This setup uses zero indicators. No RSI, no MACD, no moving averages. Pure price action and trend lines. If you can draw a line and watch price, you can trade this setup. The clarity is the advantage.

2-Touch Point Trend Line Break

A 2-touch point trend line break occurs when price has touched your trend line exactly twice before breaking through it on the third interaction.

Why Does the Touch Count Matter?

Each time price respects a trend line, it confirms that the line is "real" and that the market participants are aware of it. More touch points = more market participants have acknowledged and reacted to this level. When the line finally breaks, more participants will react to the break signal.

3-Touch Point Trend Line Break

A 3-touch point trend line break is exactly the same setup — same rules, same action and safety lines, same entry trigger — but price has respected the trend line three times before finally breaking.

💪

More Touches = Stronger Data

Think of it like a scientific experiment. The more times you run the experiment and get the same result, the more confident you are in your hypothesis. Three price respects of a trend line is stronger evidence than two. Both are valid — but 3-touch setups give you more data to work with.

The Pros of This System

✅ Advantages

  • Zero indicators needed
  • Works on any instrument
  • Crystal clear rule-based system
  • Builds patience & discipline
  • Well-defined entry and exit
  • Can be tracked scientifically

⚠️ Things to Note

  • Requires waiting for the break
  • Not every break will be profitable
  • Some instruments are harder to analyze
  • You need to track which setups work best for you

This Is the Science Experiment

By labeling your setups as "2-touch" or "3-touch," you can track which setup type performs best for your specific instrument, time frame, and market. Over time, your journal will reveal patterns: maybe 3-touch setups on crude oil outperform 2-touch setups. That's data. That's your edge becoming measurable.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 7

  • The trend line break is the core setup — the "meat of the strategy."
  • No indicators used — purely price action and trend lines.
  • 2-touch break: price respects the line twice, then breaks it.
  • 3-touch break: price respects the line three times, then breaks it.
  • More touch points = more market confirmation of the level.
  • Both setups use the same rules: action line, safety line, stop-loss beyond safety.
  • Track each setup type separately to find what works best for your market.
