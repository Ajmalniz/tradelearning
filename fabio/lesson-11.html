🔀 FLIP OF THE AUCTION

When the Market Changes Direction

The Flip of the Auction is when the dominant direction of the market reverses. It's not just a price bounce — it's a fundamental shift in who controls the auction, confirmed by specific order flow signals.

"The Flip of the Auction — an amazing example of the battle. You see buyers getting aggressive, buyers getting aggressive, sellers getting aggressive here — but they are protected by this liquidity wall. When the sellers take the final decision to breach through — this is important — because at the same time you see the protection of the aggressive market order sustained by the liquidity wall." — Fabio

🛡 BREAK & PROTECT

Sustained Breakout Confirmation Signal

The Break and Protect is the specific event WITHIN the Auction Flip that tells you the move is real — not a fake breakout. It's the single most important entry confirmation in Fabio's entire system.

The pattern: price breaks a level AND fresh liquidity immediately loads on the other side of that level (protecting the breakout).

Why Break & Protect is So Powerful

Most breakouts fail — price breaks a level, then reverses. The Break & Protect filters out the fakes:

✅ Real breakout: Price breaks above → fresh buy orders immediately appear BELOW the broken level (sellers can't get back in)

❌ Fake breakout: Price breaks above → no fresh buy orders appear below → buyers don't want to be filled here → price drifts back

The fresh reload is essentially large buyers saying: "We want to get filled even MORE at this level — the breakout is real and we're protecting it."

Fabio from the live session: "So they want to get more contracts filled to go long. This is important because this one here is protecting the intention to go higher. And in this case, if you see this one raising — oh perfect — if you see this one raising, it means that they are willing to get filled at higher levels to be in the movement."

🧲 ABSORPTION

When the Passive Wall Stops the Aggressive Wave

Absorption occurs when aggressive orders repeatedly hit a passive level and get "absorbed" — the passive orders match and neutralize the aggression without being overcome. Price stops, rejects, or reverses.

❌ Failed Absorption (Breakout)

Aggressive buyers hit sell wall → partial absorption → buyers regroup → hit it again with more force → passive orders exhausted → price breaks through. The wall failed to hold.

✅ Sustained Absorption (Reversal)

Aggressive buyers hit sell wall → absorbed → try again → absorbed → try third time → absorbed + cannot surpass the first aggression point. Wall holds. Strong reversal signal.

"You can see here that the price tried to break through but you see the first line absorption, and the second time they try to come again here, they cannot surpass the first aggression. This is a strong confirmation that this is a failed auction and the price will reverse for the path of least resistance." — Fabio

Reading Absorption from Bubbles

On the heat map, absorption appears as large circular bubbles at a single price level. Multiple large bubbles at the same level = multiple absorption attempts. This is visualized as a "horizontal absorption" — you see many large bubbles stacked at one horizontal price, showing the battle is concentrated there.

How to Use Absorption in Your Trades

As entry signal: After 2-3 absorptions at a level with your directional bias, the absorption confirms the level is holding. Enter after the third absorption in the direction of your bias.

As take profit signal: When you're in a trade and you see absorption happening at a level ahead of you (bubbles clustering at a horizontal), consider closing part or all of your position — that level is acting as resistance to your move.

As stop signal: If you're long and see heavy absorption of BUYERS (your side getting absorbed), that's a warning. The passive resistance above is stronger than your bull move. Consider reducing or exiting.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 11

  • Flip of the Auction: when the dominant side (buyers/sellers) changes. Confirmed by: breach of key level + fresh reload on the other side + sustained aggression.
  • Break & Protect: the specific confirmation signal. Breakout + fresh liquidity immediately loading below (for bull) or above (for bear) the broken level = real breakout.
  • No fresh reload after a break = fake breakout. Wait for the reload to appear before committing.
  • Absorption: aggressive orders repeatedly hit passive orders but get neutralized. Price stops or reverses here.
  • Failed absorption = breakout incoming. Sustained absorption (can't surpass first attempt) = reversal confirmation.
  • All three patterns require context from VWAP and CVD to determine which direction to trade them.

✅ Real auction flip requires: sustained aggressive orders + fresh reload in the flip direction + the broken level acts as new resistance/support. One alone isn't enough — you need all three to confirm the auction has truly changed direction.
