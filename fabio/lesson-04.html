The Liquidity Heat Map:
See the Invisible

The technology that lets professionals watch resting orders in real time — a quantum leap beyond the standard order book.

⏱ 14 min 🟡 Intermediate 🔥 Game Changer

What Makes the Heat Map Different?

You've learned from Lesson 3 that price moves by exhausting passive liquidity at each level. But to trade with this knowledge, you need to see where that passive liquidity is — in real time, across all price levels, with historical context.

The standard order book gives you a list of numbers. The Liquidity Heat Map gives you a living, visual picture of the entire order landscape. Fabio describes it:

"It's a technology step from the usual order book because it shows you also the historical movement of the liquidity and it's really visually appealing and easy to understand — you have the darker level or the level that is more powerful from a visual perspective... if the level is important, the color is more bright." — Fabio

How the Heat Map Visualizes Orders

The core principle: the more contracts resting at a price level, the brighter (more intense) the color at that level. Low liquidity levels are dim or invisible. Massive levels glow.

The Historical Dome: Seeing the Past

Unlike a regular order book that only shows the present moment, the heat map shows you a historical dome — where liquidity has been over time. This is incredibly powerful because:

💡 What the Historical Dome Tells You

If you see liquidity rising on the heat map over time (appearing at progressively higher prices), it means large players are lifting their bids — they're willing to pay more. This signals bullish intent.

If liquidity appears stacked and hasn't been consumed despite price touching it multiple times, those levels are resting liquidity with strong defending interest behind them.

The Two Types of Liquidity on the Heat Map

🔵 Resting Liquidity

Passive orders that have been sitting at a level for some time. They've been there, seen price approach, and survived. This is "old" liquidity — it signals a strong, deliberate position that someone is defending.

🟢 Fresh Liquidity

New passive orders that just appeared. When fresh liquidity appears right after a breakout — especially in the direction of the move — it means someone is actively adding protection to fuel a sustained move. This is the most powerful signal.

Fresh liquidity added immediately after a breakout = high probability of a sustained, continued move in that direction. This is what professionals look for before committing to a trade.

The Two Interactive Forces on the Heat Map

At every moment on the heat map, you see the interaction of:

  • Bubbles (aggressive market orders) — visible as large circular indicators showing where aggressive buying/selling occurred
  • Passive liquidity levels — the colored bars at each price showing resting orders

When bubbles approach a liquidity level, one of two things happens: absorption (the level holds and absorbs the aggression) or breakthrough (the aggressive orders exhaust the passive orders and price moves through).

Platform Settings: How to Read the Heat Map Optimally

Fabio uses the DeepDome platform. Here's how he configures it for maximum clarity:

  • 1
    Contrast Setting: Higher = Cleaner

    Set contrast high (e.g., 80%) so only the most important levels glow. Weak levels fade to invisible. This prevents information overload — you only see levels that actually matter.

  • 2
    Bubble Size: Medium for Long Sessions

    Medium bubble size is easiest on the eyes for multi-hour scalping sessions. Large bubbles can feel overwhelming; small can be hard to spot quickly.

  • 3
    Bubble Grouping: 70-75% for Absorption Trading

    Setting bubble grouping to 70-75% makes it easy to spot where buyers or sellers get absorbed at a level — large horizontal bubbles become immediately visible.

  • 4
    Mark the Most Important Levels

    Use the drawing tools to mark key levels: where sellers last took control, where buyers last took control, and major intermediate liquidity levels. This keeps your focus on the battle zones.

Why This Gives You an Edge Over 95% of Traders

The Information Asymmetry

While retail traders are drawing support/resistance lines and hoping price stops there, you will KNOW:

• Exactly how many contracts are at each price level
• Whether those levels are fresh or old
• Whether liquidity is growing or shrinking as price approaches
• Which direction has more passive resistance (path of least resistance)
• Whether large players are adding protective liquidity to a move

This is not predicting. This is reading directly from the exchange's own data.

"These data are provided directly from the exchange. So you have one certainty — you know for a fact that these data are real and these are actual levels waiting to get filled, paired with actual execution on the bubble." — Fabio

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 4

  • The heat map visualizes resting orders by brightness — brighter color = more contracts = stronger level.
  • The historical dome shows movement over time, not just the current snapshot.
  • Resting liquidity = old, survived levels. Fresh liquidity = new, just-added levels.
  • Fresh liquidity appearing after a breakout = high probability of sustained continuation.
  • Data comes directly from the exchange — it's real, not interpreted or assumed.
  • You see TWO things: passive levels (bars) and aggressive orders (bubbles). Their interaction tells the story.
