The One Thing You Must Know

Before you ever look at a chart pattern, a moving average, or a candlestick, there is one concept that underlies every single movement in every financial market on earth. That concept is liquidity.

So What Exactly IS Liquidity?

Liquidity simply means: orders waiting to be filled in the market. Every time someone places a trade, they either:

  1. Add liquidity — place a limit order that sits and waits (passive)
  2. Remove liquidity — place a market order that immediately matches with a waiting order (aggressive)

Price moves when aggressive orders run out of passive orders at one level and need to go to the next level to get filled. That's it. That's the entire mechanism of price movement.

Why Retail Traders Get It Wrong

Most retail traders use a simplified version of liquidity. They say:

  • Stop-losses rest above recent highs (buy-side liquidity)
  • Stop-losses rest below recent lows (sell-side liquidity)
  • Market makers "hunt" these levels

This is not wrong — but it's only accurate 60-70% of the time. Why? Because you're guessing where liquidity is. You don't actually know.

⚠️

The Retail Trap

When you assume liquidity sits above a high or below a low, you might be completely ignoring a massive liquidity level that is RIGHT THERE on the book — because you lack the tools to see it. You're predicting. A professional is reading.

The Two Forces in Every Market

Every single tick, every single trade, every single movement in any financial market is the result of two forces meeting:

🟢

Aggressive Orders (Bubbles)

Market orders. They say "fill me NOW." They move price. They are willing to pay the spread. They eat passive liquidity.

🔵

Passive Orders (Liquidity)

Limit orders. They wait. They provide liquidity. They get eaten. Their size and location determines WHERE price goes.

The aggressive market orders (bubbles) match with passive liquidity — and this is HOW the market moves and the exchange happens. Understanding this is the entire foundation of professional trading.

Why This Changes Everything

Once you understand liquidity, you stop asking "where will price go?" and start asking "where is the passive liquidity that will stop or propel this move?" These are completely different questions — and the second one has a real, objective answer you can read from the market itself.

💡

The Professional Mindset Shift

A retail trader draws lines and hopes. A professional reads the order book and knows — with high probability — where price is attracted to, where it will be rejected, and which direction offers the path of least resistance right now.
