The Core Principles This Reveals

This simple example contains the entire truth of market movement. Let's extract the principles:

Principle 1
Price Moves When Demand Exceeds Supply at a Level
When aggressive orders exhaust all passive liquidity at one price, they must step to the next level. THIS is price movement — nothing more, nothing less.
Principle 2
Large Passive Levels Slow or Stop Price
If 103 had 500 contracts instead of 31, the 75 aggressive buyers couldn't have moved through it. Price would have stalled. More passive liquidity = stronger level.
Principle 3
You Cannot Choose Where You Get Filled if You're Aggressive
Our 75 buyers got filled at 101, 102, AND 103 — not just where they wanted. They paid worse prices as they consumed more liquidity. This is called "slippage."
Principle 4
The Heat Map Shows You Where Price Will Struggle
If you could see that 103 had only 31 contracts but 104 had 500+, you'd know exactly where the next major resistance was. That's the power of the liquidity heat map.

Price Certainty vs Execution Certainty

This simulation perfectly illustrates Fabio's key teaching about the two types of certainty:

🔵 Passive Order (Limit)

Price certainty: You know EXACTLY what price you'll get.

No execution certainty: Price might never reach your limit. You could wait forever and never get filled.

No time certainty: You have zero control over when you get filled.

🔴 Aggressive Order (Market)

Execution certainty: You WILL be filled immediately.

No price certainty: You get whatever prices are available — you might be filled across multiple levels.

Time certainty: Filled right now, always.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 3

  • Price moves when aggressive orders exhaust passive liquidity at one level and step to the next.
  • Large passive liquidity levels act as walls — strong resistance or support. More contracts = stronger wall.
  • 75 buyers moving price from 100→103 is the exact mechanical process behind every candlestick you've ever seen.
  • Passive orders offer price certainty but no execution or time certainty.
  • Aggressive orders offer execution certainty but no price certainty — they pay whatever the book demands.
  • The liquidity heat map shows you WHERE these passive walls are BEFORE price reaches them. That's your edge.

A) A market maker decided to push price up
B) The 75 aggressive buyers exhausted all passive sell liquidity at 101 and 102, forcing price to step to 103 to complete fills
C) There was breaking news that drove the market up
D) A chart pattern triggered a breakout
✅ Exactly right! Price steps up because aggressive demand exceeds passive supply level by level. No conspiracy, no chart magic — just mechanics.

2. If 103 had 500 contracts instead of 31, what would have happened?

A) Price would have moved even higher, past 103
B) Price would have stalled at 103 — the massive passive wall would absorb the 75 aggressive buyers completely
C) Nothing would change
D) Price would have reversed down immediately
✅ A 500-contract wall at 103 would absorb all 75 buyers and price would stall there. This is the principle behind "liquidity cluster walls" — they stop or reverse price.

3. A trader places a limit sell order at 105. They have:

A) Price certainty (they'll sell at exactly 105 if filled) but no execution certainty (price may never reach 105)
B) Execution certainty and price certainty
C) No certainty at all
D) Execution certainty but no price certainty
✅ Limit orders give price certainty (you set the price) but no execution certainty. If price never reaches 105, your order sits waiting forever.
