🍖 THE GRILL

Eating Through Stacked Liquidity Levels

Credit to Enrico "Ricotoi" Stoki who named this pattern. The Grill — also called the Barbecue — is when price aggressively moves through multiple stacked liquidity levels one by one, consuming them like a grill eating through food.

"The Grill — the barbecue — when you have multiple liquidity areas and the price gets aggressive here to eat all this level of liquidity. You can see from the aggressive market order that it is pushing really hard to slice through them because this is a perfect example of a sustained move where you have aggression, aggression, aggression and the price is eating all the passive liquidity level really aggressively." — Fabio

How to Trade the Grill

Identify Multiple Stacked Levels Above (for long) or Below (for short)

Look for 3+ passive liquidity levels stacked relatively close together on the heat map. This is "fuel" for the grill pattern.

See the First Aggressive Push Through Level 1

Price breaks through the first level with large aggressive bubbles. NOT a quiet drift — a forceful, large-candle move.

Confirm: Aggression Continues Through Level 2

If price also breaks the second level with the same energy, this is confirmation of the Grill pattern. The sustained aggression is the signature.

Take Partial Profits at Each Level

"These are all partial profits that you can take because the market usually reaches a point of exhaustion." Take some at each level while keeping the core position running.

Exit at the Final Cluster (Exhaustion)

When price reaches a dense cluster of multiple levels stacked together, this is where exhaustion and reversal happens. Close the full position here.

⚡ STOOKIE GRILL

Opening Session Drive with Protective Reloads

The Stookie Grill is named after Enrico Stoki. It's the Grill's cousin — most commonly seen at the opening of the New York session — with one key additional feature: protective fresh liquidity loads at each level as price pushes through.

"This is usually during the opening. You can see that they protect their movement. So they do the first push — put fresh liquidity to protect. Here they do the second push and they put fresh liquidity to fuel. This means they want to go up. They want to capitalize up." — Fabio

Key distinction: Notice the fresh buy reloads appear at the bottom of each push — not at the level being broken above. This is "protective" liquidity — it's being placed BELOW the current price to prevent any retracement from gaining ground. The market is aggressively "protecting its gains" at each step.

What "Empty Space Above" Means

In the Stookie Grill, the fresh liquidity appears BELOW (protection), but there is often empty space above the broken levels. Fabio notes: "they are not putting liquidity here — this is empty. They are going to put protective fresh liquidity to fuel the aggression on the way up."

Empty space above = no resistance remaining between current price and the final target cluster. Price will accelerate through empty zones.

🧱 CLUSTER WALL

The Strongest Resistance Zone in the Market

A Liquidity Cluster Wall is when multiple passive liquidity levels are stacked closely together on the same side, creating a combined resistance zone far stronger than any single level.

"When you see that there is a strong cluster of four levels stacked on each other — way higher than this aggressive liquidity of the sellers — all these levels on the NASDAQ... you can understand that the highest probability here is that they will get a full fill." — Fabio

The Counterintuitive Cluster Wall Insight

When you see a cluster wall, your first instinct might be "lots of sell orders = go short." But Fabio says the opposite is often true in the right context:

"You can see the absorption on this level to go to the next liquidity level on the way up."

Why? Because the cluster wall represents a target for the aggressive buyers — they WANT to fill those levels. If buyers have enough momentum and the path between them and the cluster is clear (empty or thin), the highest probability is that price goes TO the cluster to fill all those orders.

The cluster wall is a magnet, not just a barrier.

When to Fade the Cluster Wall

The cluster wall becomes a reversal zone when price reaches it and you see ABSORPTION — aggressive buyers push into the cluster and get absorbed. At this point:

• First try: absorption (price bounces slightly)
• Second try: price cannot surpass the first aggression point
• "This is a strong confirmation that this is a failed auction and the price will reverse for the path of least resistance."

After absorption at the cluster wall, it becomes the strongest resistance level in the market — and a premium short entry.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 10

  • The Grill: price cuts through multiple stacked levels with sustained aggression. Take partials at each level, full exit at the final cluster (exhaustion).
  • The Stookie Grill: opening session version with protective fresh reloads below each push level. Empty space above = price accelerates.
  • Liquidity Cluster Wall: multiple levels stacked on one side. Can be both a TARGET (price attracted to fill them) and a REVERSAL ZONE (once absorbed, strongest resistance).
  • Cluster wall context matters: if price is moving TOWARD the cluster, it's a magnet. If price reaches it and gets absorbed, it becomes a reversal zone.
  • The Grill and Stookie Grill are sustained move patterns — you need strong directional bias before entering.
  • Always remember: the market is the path of least resistance. Clusters can be passed through, not just blocked by.
