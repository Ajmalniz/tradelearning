Reload Patterns

The core heat map setups — when passive liquidity reloads AND aggression appears simultaneously. The highest-probability entry signal in Fabio's system.

⏱ 16 min 🔴 Advanced 🎯 High Probability

The Core Principle: Reload + Aggression = Signal

The reload patterns are the foundation of Fabio's actual trading setups. These occur on the heat map when you see TWO things happening at the same time at a level:

  1. Passive liquidity reloads at the level (resting orders added — fresh or refreshed)
  2. Aggressive orders appear in the same direction as your bias (bubbles showing conviction)

One alone is interesting. Both together is a high-probability trade signal.

BUYERS RELOAD

Long Bias + Protection Wall Pattern

The Buyers Reload occurs when you have a long bias for the day and you see the following on the heat map:

  • A large resting buy liquidity wall below price (protection wall)
  • Price approaches the wall and gets absorbed (buyers there absorb the sellers)
  • Buyers then reload passive liquidity AND show aggressive buying bubbles

How the Buyers Reload Plays Out

  • 1
    You Have a Long Bias

    VWAP says buyers in control. CVD rising. You want to buy, but need a precise entry with low risk.

  • 2
    Price Approaches the Protection Wall

    Price dips toward the resting buy liquidity wall. This wall has been there a while — it's resting, not fresh. It's defended.

  • 3
    First Absorption

    Sellers hit the wall. The wall absorbs them — price doesn't break. Wall holds. First confirmation.

  • 4
    Second and Third Absorption

    "The market approaches here and gets absorbed the first time, strikes again this area, absorbed the second time, strikes this area, absorbed the third time." — Each rejection confirms the wall's strength.

  • 5
    Buyers Reload Fresh + Get Aggressive

    Fresh buy liquidity appears ABOVE the wall. Aggressive buy bubbles appear. This is the double confirmation — passive protection AND active conviction. ENTER LONG.

  • 6
    Price Extends Higher

    Price moves toward the next liquidity levels above — your take profit targets. Stop loss is below the protection wall.

Fabio's Words on the Buyers Reload

"You need to start with an idea. But for scalpers, where you want to first see the market getting aggressive on your direction — so you want to see maybe big trades confirming that there is an aggression there and that there is intention to go higher."

Two sub-patterns exist:
• Mean Revert to the Wall: Price comes back to the protection wall and bounces. Enter in direction of main trend.
• Breakout Reload: Close to a level with buyers reloading AND getting aggressive — enter the breakout protected below the reload level.

SELLERS RELOAD

The Sellers Reload in Detail

Fabio's description: "We have a market that is closed inside a resting liquidity fresh area of the buyers and is protected on the aggression from the sellers. So who is pushing the market? The sellers are getting aggressive here. They are trying to break through this level."

  • 1
    Sellers Are Getting Aggressive

    Aggressive sell bubbles appear. Sellers are pushing price down from a fresh buyers zone (resistance ceiling above).

  • 2
    Sellers Reload Fresh Liquidity

    "At the same time they reload fresh liquidity. This is an information that they want to fuel the movement to the next liquidity levels."

  • 3
    Price Reaches Target, Comes Back, Reloads Again

    "They come back to the level. They reload again and they get aggressive again protected with aggressive orders." The pattern repeats multiple times.

  • 4
    Full Breakdown

    After confirming 2-4 times, the sellers finally have enough fuel. Price makes a sustained move to TP1 and TP2 liquidity levels.

Important Caution for the Sellers Reload

"You always need to be careful because the price usually goes to the next liquidity cluster. And the next liquidity cluster in this case was really close. So the risk-to-reward is not the best."

Always check: where is TP1 relative to your entry? If the next liquidity cluster is only 5 ticks away but your stop is 10 ticks, skip the trade. The pattern must offer at least 1:1 R:R — ideally 1:2 or better.

⚡ Key Takeaways from Lesson 9

  • The reload pattern requires TWO simultaneous signals: passive liquidity reload + aggressive orders in same direction.
  • Buyers Reload: resting wall absorbs sellers 1-3 times → buyers reload fresh + show aggression → enter long above the wall.
  • Sellers Reload: sellers get aggressive + reload fresh liquidity → enter short → price moves to next liquidity targets.
  • Always start with directional bias (VWAP + CVD) BEFORE looking for the reload pattern.
  • Check R:R before every trade — the next liquidity target must be far enough to justify the stop.
  • The pattern can confirm 2-4 times before the final move — each confirmation increases probability.

Knowledge Check

Test your understanding of Buyers and Sellers Reload Patterns

1. You see a big resting buy wall below price. Sellers try to break it 3 times but get absorbed each time. Then fresh buy liquidity appears AND aggressive buy bubbles show up. This is:

A) A sell signal — all that selling pressure means the market is going down
B) A Buyers Reload pattern — high probability long entry with stop below the protection wall, targeting the next liquidity levels above
C) An iceberg pattern — ignore it
D) A spoofing attempt — the reload will disappear
✅ This is the textbook Buyers Reload: wall absorbs sellers (3 tests = strong confirmation), then fresh buy reload + aggression = double confirmation. Enter long, stop below the wall, target next liquidity levels.

2. In the Sellers Reload, what does it mean when sellers "reload fresh liquidity"?

A) Sellers are buying back their short positions
B) New passive sell orders appear at the level, signaling that sellers want MORE contracts filled and intend to fuel a continued downward move
C) The sell orders were cancelled and fresh ones placed far away
D) The market is about to gap up
✅ Fresh sell reload = sellers want more short exposure filled. They're adding passive sell orders to get filled at the current level, which tells you they intend to drive price much lower. This is fuel, not a warning.

A) Yes — any sellers reload is automatically worth taking
B) No — the R:R is only 1:1 and the target is too close. Look for a setup where TP1 is at least 2× the stop distance away (1:2 R:R or better)
C) Yes — sellers reload always results in a 100-tick move
D) Only take it if CVD shows sellers are dominating
✅ 1:1 R:R with targets that close is poor. Fabio warns: "the next liquidity cluster was really close, so the risk-to-reward is not the best." Skip setups with poor R:R even when the pattern looks perfect.
