The Complete 6-Step Framework

Everything you've learned in this course flows into this framework. Follow these six steps in order on every single trade. Never skip a step.

1

Build Your Directional Bias

Before touching the heat map, answer: which direction does the market favor right now?

  • VWAP position: above = bullish bias, below = bearish bias
  • CVD direction: rising = buyers in control, falling = sellers
  • Statistical projections, IVB, or pivot levels for the session
  • Higher timeframe structure (are we in a bull or bear trend?)
2

Map the Heat Map Landscape

Open the liquidity heat map and identify the key levels:

  • Major liquidity walls in both directions
  • Fresh vs resting liquidity — which is stronger?
  • Where is the path of least resistance? (Compare liquidity above vs below)
  • Mark your "hot zones" — the levels price is most likely to visit
3

Wait for the Pattern Signal

With bias set and key levels marked, wait for a heat map pattern to confirm:

  • Buyers/Sellers Reload: passive reload + aggression in your direction
  • Break & Protect: breakout + fresh reload on protection side
  • Absorption at a key level: failed auction confirmed with 2-3 tests
  • Book Sweep at session/range extreme for asymmetric trades
4

Time Your Entry with Order Flow

Don't enter on anticipation — enter on confirmation:

  • Wait for aggressive bubbles to appear in your direction (conviction)
  • Enter on the break (for breakout trades) or after the absorption (for reversal)
  • For scalpers: "first see the market getting aggressive on your direction" — Fabio
  • Accept some additional costs for confirmation vs guessing early
5

Place Your Stop Loss

Always an AREA — not a single tick level:

  • Below the reload level / protection wall for longs
  • Above the reload level / resistance wall for shorts
  • Below the nearest passive liquidity cluster (never between levels)
  • Remember: "it's not a level — it's always an area. There are orders around it."
6

Manage the Trade & Take Profits

Active management using the heat map throughout the trade:

  • TP1: first major liquidity level in your direction
  • TP2: second liquidity cluster or the session's major target
  • Trail stop to reload levels as price confirms continuation (Book Flip)
  • Watch CVD: if it flips against you, reduce size or exit
  • Watch for absorption at levels ahead — those are your partials points

The Golden Rules of This System

✅

Always Start with Bias

Never trade from the heat map alone. VWAP and CVD determine direction FIRST. The heat map only provides precision entry timing.

❌

Never Fight the CVD

"I will not try to get long while the sellers are dominating the auction because it's a suicide." Wait for CVD to align with your intended direction.

📐

It's Always an Area

"Remember, it's not a level — it's always an area. It's important to understand this." Stops and entries are areas of liquidity, not single ticks.

⚖️

Check R:R Before Every Trade

Even a perfect pattern with poor risk-to-reward is a skip. TP1 must be at least 2× your stop distance. No exceptions.

🔄

Fresh Reload = Fuel

When fresh liquidity loads in your direction after a breakout, it's fuel for the move. When it appears against you, it's a warning. Track fresh vs resting constantly.

🚨

Respect the Higher Timeframe

"Remember that this is market micro mechanics. You always need a confirmation from the higher timeframe on what's happening here." Micro mechanics confirm the macro, not the other way around.
